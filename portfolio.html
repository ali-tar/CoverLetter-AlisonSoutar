Wearable Design: Considerations for Children's Smart Watches

Designing digital products for children is a difficult and unmediated task. One must find suitable ways to simplify complex interactions and tasks into visual (and, depending on age demographic, para-verbal) mediums can prove challenging. In this project, my team and I evaluated several ways we could design a children's health monitoring application, taking into consideration: - Physical restraints (watch size), - Legal restraints (children's safety), - Comprehension restraints (children's understanding), and - Desired functionality (user needs).

Refining the Cold Calling Process: Freelance UX/UI Application Design

A local Real Estate company contacted me to improve their cold calling process by designing a solution to monitor and manage various "sheets" of client and apartment block information. Their current system involves a variety of Google Sheets, which has major security and efficiency concerns. Users need to gather client information on one sheet, and mark down the result of a call on another. The goal was to create an application with different user types thereby controlling the flow of data and prevent information theft, while improving the user experience and simplifying the process.

E-Commerce: Maintaining Customer Attention from Browsing to Check-Out

During our collaboration with UX Company MakeReign, we were given a task to exercise our design and research skills. Using TotalSports, a local brand, we were asked to give the product view page a complete facelift in accordance with user feedback, design standards, and user interface principles. We redesigned both the mobile and web versions - focusing on mobile-first development. The result was a well-refined, simplified and sleek final design, prioritising product view and check-out process - thereby shifting focus on to checking-out (thereby increasing revenue) over browsing.

IS03: Final Application Prototype and Design

The festival scene in South Africa is growing at a rapid pace, quickly becoming one of the largest attractions for tourists and locals alike. Event news is often communicated through word of mouth of over social media - Often leading to users missing out on festivals that suit their interests, as well as organisers struggling to reach wider audiences if they have poor social media presence. This phase of the project summarises both IS01 and IS02 and proposes a final suggestion to the proposed problem, with in-depth market research, user testing and iterative prototype development.

Creating Children's E-Reader: They're Smarter Than We Think

Reading books to children under six is an essential part of early childhood development. Libraries and physical bookstores are great resources for this – However, the experience of finding, selecting, and downloading the e-books can be cumbersome. Additionally, the reader applications are generally designed for older individuals reading alone. In this case study, we will address the problems prevalent in the existing methods of reading e-books to children and discuss and illustrate improvements to these methods, resulting in an improved design.
